Emmeline Pankhurst

She became involved in women's suffrage in 1880 and formed the Women's Social & Political Union (WSPU) when her local branch of the Independent Labour Party refused to admit women members. In 1879 Emmeline Goulden married Richard Marsden Pankhurst, lawyer, friend of John Stuart Mill, and author of the first woman suffrage bill in Great Britain (late 1860s) and of the Married Women Property acts (1870, 1882). Ten years later she founded the Women Franchise League, which secured (1894) for married women the right to vote in elections to local offices (not to the House of Commons). From 1895 she held a succession of municipal offices in Manchester, but her energies were increasingly in demand by the Women Social and Political Union (WSPU), which she founded in 1903 in Manchester. The union first attracted wide attention on October 13, 1905, when two of its members, Christabel Pankhurst and Annie Kenney, thrown out of a Liberal Party meeting for demanding a statement about votes for women, were arrested in the street for a technical assault on the police and, after refusing to pay fines, were sent to prison.

Marcus Rashford

Marcus Rashford MBE is an English professional footballer who plays as a forward for Premier League club Manchester United and the England national team. A product of the Manchester United youth system, Rashford joined the club at the age of seven.Marcus is both committed and passionate about his campaign to the point of becoming an ambassador of FareShare. FareShare works to support vulnerable children at risk of hunger over the summer holidays. This is an issue very close to Marcus’s heart. Marcus grew up in a home where they relied on free school meals and at times on food banks.Marcus's dedication and enthusiasm towards his campaign have made him an ambassador for FareShare, showcasing his unwavering commitment. FareShare aims to assist underprivileged children who face the risk of hunger during the summer break. This cause holds immense significance for Marcus, as he himself grew up in a household that relied on free school meals and occasionally turned to food banks.Childhood: Marcus grew up in Wythenshawe, an area in the city of Manchester. During his childhood, he resided with his mother, Melanie Maynard, and his four older siblings. Despite his mother's hard work, Marcus experienced child poverty, often facing a shortage of food. To cope with this, the family relied on food banks, breakfast clubs, and free school meals. Marcus recalls receiving free food from his mother's workplace, which helped him understand the value of generosity. Additionally, he admired Ronaldo, the renowned Portuguese footballer, during his formative years. Family: In order to ensure that Marcus and his siblings had enough to eat, his mother often had to work multiple jobs, sometimes sacrificing her own meals. Marcus has two brothers, Dwaine Maynard and Dane Rashford, who now represent him professionally. He also has two sisters named Chantelle and Claire.
